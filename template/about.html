Why Brains and Foods?

Meet the project and its developer.

The What

There has not been much work done in Brain Computer Interface (BCI) devices paired with food. Human beings need to eat, both for sustained existence and for pleasure, and there is much to be said with how the human brain determines what it “wants” to eat. We all have different tastes at different times - but why?

The Why

That being said, picking what a person wants to eat has long been a fight between groups of friends, families, and significant others. As someone who avoids conflict at all costs, these fights have always seemed so crazy and unnecessary. Why not try to solve it? With a passion for brains, food, and programming, that's where I come in.

The Who

My name is Sara Lichtenstein. I am a senior at the University of Florida studying computer science engineering. I chose this as my senior project because I want to pioneer this fight - the fight to know what we want, when we want it. Going into my 20s, this has been a challenge in so many other areas; why should food be just as hard? Granted, I always want pizza, but I'd also like to know why, and why some people don't (seriously, some people hate pizza).
